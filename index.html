National Membership

Nu Rho Psi is the National Honor Society in Neuroscience, founded in 2006 by the Faculty for Undergraduate Neuroscience. Nu Rho Psi is an independent non-profit, grass-roots organization comprised of neuroscientists, like you!

Paths at USC

At the University of Southern California, Nu Rho Psi serves as the premier hub for academic, professional, and fun neuroscience scholars. Nu Rho Psi systemizes students across disciplines to explore and dive into neuroscience research, internships, and pre-med outlooks.

Meet Us
Leadership at USC A team of motivated neuroscience lovers trying to make Neuroscience more fun, accessible, and full of possibilities for the students of University of Southern California. Meet the Leadership Dr. David McKemy Peripheral somatosensory neurons critically differentiate innocuous stimuli from those that cause tissue damage and pain (nociception). We use a combination of cellular, genetic, and behavioral approaches to understand how these cells transduce these discrete environmental stimuli, and their contribution to inflammatory and neuropathic pain. Meet Dr. McKemy Dr. Dion Dickman Synapse development, function, and plasticity using a combination of Drosophila genetics, confocal imaging, and electrophysiological approaches.

Meet Dr. Dickman Dr. MacLean Research in our group focuses on developing mathematical and computational methods to study stem cell fate. Ultimately, we seek to derive a mechanistic theory for stem cell function in health and disease. To shed light on these complex regulatory mechanisms, we build models and develop methods based in dynamical systems theory, statistical inference, high dimensional data analysis, and systems biology.
